Website Evaluations

The North Face

The North Face website stands out for its clean layout, strong product imagery, and consistent branding. It feels modern, straightforward, and user-friendly.

  • Contrast: Their bold product images pop against a predominantly white or light-gray background, making text and calls-to-action easy to spot.
  • Repetition: Fonts, colors, and button styles are consistent across each page. Navigation elements and headlines all share the same design language.
  • Alignment: Content grids are aligned neatly, keeping product listings and promotional banners clean and organized. Headings and text blocks follow a clear left or center alignment.
  • Proximity: Related information (like product descriptions, pricing, and “Add to Cart” buttons) is grouped together, making it easy for users to find what they need without excessive scrolling.

Arngren

Arngren is a classic example of chaotic web design, with jumbled images, mismatched color palettes, and an overwhelming amount of text scattered all over the page.

  • Contrast: The site bombards visitors with clashing colors, bright GIFs, and uncoordinated images, so text often competes visually with background elements.
  • Repetition: It lacks a consistent style-fonts, colors, and layout elements vary wildly from one section to the next.
  • Alignment: Content boxes, images, and text appear randomly placed, so there is little sense of a structured grid or purposeful layout.
  • Proximity: Elements that belong together (like products, pricing, and descriptions) are scattered in different places, forcing users to hunt around.
